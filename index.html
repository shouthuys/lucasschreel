About

My passion for music was born at the music academy in Lennik, where I studied with Helen Borremans and Philip Bruffaerts. The latter was one of my many excellent tutors at the Kunsthumaniora Brussel, where I continued to study music more in depth.

I then went on to focus on classical guitar at the Royal Conservatory of Ghent with Yves Storms and Johan Fostier. Complemented with composition courses by Lucien Postman and Frank Nuyts. Afterwards I took my degree in Pedagogics at KISP CVO Gent.

On the side, I took electric guitar/improvisation courses with Frederik Leroux, who teaches jazz guitar at the Conservatory in Antwerp.

Currently I am a dedicated classical guitar teacher in Academy of Sint Agatha Berchem (Brussels) and SAMWD Sint-Niklaas.

As a musician I'm in to lots of different kinds of music.
Most of my personal projects have a experimental touch. Kloothommel is a lo-fi experimental indierock band, Schmeeks is an experimental singer-songwriter project that combines very expressive passages with noise breaks for instance.
Also improvisation is a key element in my music. Currently working on a new project that combines jazz orientated sounds with (noise)rock, where impro has a strong importance.

Besides experiment or breaking boundaries I also enjoy musical conventions or more standard sounds. In this context I have recent experience as a pop guitarist with Amery.

I did masterclasses and Workshops with:
Joelle Leandre, Yannis Kyriakides, Mark Applebaum, Fabrizio Cassol, Serge Verstockt, Tom Pauwels, Jef Neve, Philip Catherine, Eric Melaerts,...

As a teacher I have work experience in: SLAC Conservatorium Leuven, Academie Aalst, SAMWD Harelbeke, Academie Groot-Bijgaarden, Muziekschool Lochristi, Muziekschool Wondelgem.
